What's In A Name

man·ĝan·to /mand͡ʒa'ntɔ/

In Esperanto, "manĝanto", meaning "eater", is the root word of "formikmanĝanto", meaning "anteater".

This name has been chosen to reflect the three ideals of our team:

Tenacity

Ants are one of the most tenacious animals on the planet. One example of this is South American fire ants, who work together to form a living raft when placed in water. Like ants, our team strives to be resilient and to work together through all challenges and problems we may face.

Cutting Edge

While ants are tenacious, anteaters swiftly feed on them by licking up as many of them as possible as quickly as possible. Like the anteater, our team will strive to be on the cutting edge, always one (or more) steps ahead of our competitors.

Universality

Esperanto is the most widely spoken constructed language, developed to facilitate communication and foster harmony between people from different countries. Our name is in Esperanto to reflect this mission to keep inclusivity at the forefront of our mind to meet the needs of a diverse customer base.
